Work Experience

Murray Engineering, P.C., New York, NY

July 2011 - July 2016

While a member of the Murray Engineering team, I had the opportunity to design numerous buildings, ranging from new construction in both residential and commercial/institutional fields to rehabilitation and adaptive reuse. Murray Engineering is a small structural design firm in New York City which specializes in these types of projects. Because of the small size of the firm, I was exposed to all aspects of the design process: conceptual design, detailed design, meetings with owners/architects/contractors, project management/proposal writing, and managing projects during construction phase. A few examples of projects I worked on while part of Murray Engineering are outlined below.

Lake Charles Memorial Hospital, Lake Charles, LA


In this project, we designed a new addition to and renovation of the existing Emergency Department. One of the challenging aspects was the canopy for the ED entrance, which consisted of a long double-cantilever corner (approx. 20-30 foot cantilever in each direction). Due to the high wind loads in the area, we had to balance appropriate beam sizes to reduce deflection with the desire of the architects to have an aesthetically pleasing slim edge to the canopy. We solved this problem by designing tapered beams which had sufficient depth for strength and deflection control near the support (area of maximum moment), and a slim profile at the edge. (link)

The Sorting House, New York, NY


This project involved designing a 3-story addition on top of the existing USPS Radio City Post Office in NYC. As the lead structural engineer on the project, I was responsible for the complete design and detailing of the addition. The unique and challenging aspect of this project was that the vertical expansion was limited due to zoning restrictions for building height in the area. To overcome this, our team proposed removing the existing roof and floating a new 3rd floor over the existing floor slab of the post office space. To reduce the weight of the new floor slab, we proposed a high density structural foam which floated over the existing floor (shown below). The owners and architect loved the idea and appreciated that we were able to carefully consider the structural implications of such an endeavor. (link)

Hauser & Wirth Art Gallery, New York, NY


This project was a personal favorite of mine. The project involved the conversion of an existing roller disco rink into a contemporary art gallery. As a child, I used to go to a local roller rink to roller skate with my family every week, so this project was full of nostalgia. Prior to commencing construction, the design team held one last "roller skating night" on the existing floor. Our primary involvement was to assist with redesigning the entrance stairs and office spaces. Once the gallery was completed, we also assisted with analyzing the existing floor structure to ensure it was capable of supporting the weight of the proposed art sculptures. (link)

KD Bridge, Rhinebeck, NY


This project invovled the restoration of an existing private access bridge on a farm in Rhinebeck, NY. The unique aspect of this project is that we were able to reuse the existing piers by casting a new pier cap on top of them, and we repurposed existing timber from another building on the property for the primary support structure, helping create the "old fashioned" asthetic of the completed bridge.
